Data-quality finding — purchase tagging gap confirmed site-wide (live pull)

The internal_referrer parameter is passed correctly through begin_checkout. It partially reaches checkout (3 events / 3 sessions attributed, but with reduced fidelity), and it is completely absent from purchase events site-wide. This was verified against the full event population for the last 30 days — not just the banner’s traffic:

checkout events (site-wide, 30d, all traffic): • (not set) 15,923 events / 11,560 sessions • "" (blank) 39 events / 20 sessions • transfers_banner 3 events / 3 sessions ← partial signal only purchase events (site-wide, 30d, all traffic): • (not set) 9,300 events / 7,377 sessions • "" (blank) 36 events / 14 sessions • transfers_banner NOT PRESENT — zero rows returned at this stage

What this means: The purchase GTM tag does not pass internal_referrer at all, for any traffic source. The 3 checkout sessions attributed to transfers_banner are a partial/inconsistent signal. Zero attributable revenue is a tagging limitation, not evidence that banner traffic didn’t convert — we simply cannot know from GA4 alone until the purchase tag is fixed.

Insights & recommended actions
Fix first
The purchase GTM tag does not pass internal_referrer at all — confirmed across the full site (zero rows for transfers_banner in 9,336 purchase events, 30 days). The checkout tag passes it partially (3 of 15,962 attributed non-banner checkout sessions carry it). Until both tags are fixed, banner-to-revenue attribution is impossible for this or any future campaign using this parameter.
Strong signal
Banner sessions convert from click to search at 85.5% vs. the site average of 34.7% — 2.5× higher. This is a robust, already-visible signal over 30 days across 62 attributed sessions. Banner clickers are meaningfully higher-intent than the typical blog visitor. This is independent of the tagging issue.
Volume caveat
Even once the purchase tag is fixed, 12 begin_checkout sessions is thin. At the site’s 2.13% overall conversion rate (session to purchase), reliably detecting a signal requires roughly 150–200 attributed sessions so that 3–5+ purchases become statistically likely. Re-evaluate when volume crosses that threshold.
Concentration risk
The Alicante→Benidorm page dominates click volume. Over 30 days, 15 unique URLs have triggered the banner but the top page still accounts for a disproportionate share. Results so far are not representative of the banner concept across the site — treat overall conclusions as provisional until volume spreads to more pages.
Mobile-majority
64% of banner sessions (43 of 67) are on mobile. Worth a QA pass to confirm the banner renders above the fold, loads quickly, and is easily tappable on small screens, since that’s where the bulk of the traffic sits.
Channel mix
58% of sessions come from Organic Search (39/67), followed by Paid Search (14, 21%). This is a warm, already-engaged audience reading transfer guides — a strong fit for the banner intent. Paid Search share has grown (5 sessions → 14 in 30-day window vs. the 7-day reference) — worth monitoring whether that’s expanding to more pages or concentrating on the same ones.
